A North East based tech meetup

NE Dev is the quarterly north east developer meetup, based in Middlesborough which brings developers, engineers and designers together to share, learn and be inspired by each other.

NEDEV v6.0


Microservices: Why Wouldn’t You? — Andrew Hatch

Splitting monolithic systems into smaller micro-services improves autonomy and encapsulation. This gives us composable and re-usable components to build a service-oriented architecture. Real life isn't clean and tidy, and certainly doesn't care for shiny tech. This talk explores the reality of working with micro-services, how we build new ones, how we break apart monoliths, and what side-effects there are to manage.

Almost Useful: An introduction to 3D printers — Nick Smith

Covering the basics of the technology, what they can do for you, and why you almost certainly shouldn’t buy one (yet)

Short Talks:

Small footprint JavaScript — Stephen James

Tired of bloated code written in mainstream over-engineered frameworks? Long digest cycles and poor performance... Tempted to just go Vanilla, but then worried you'll lose it all? In this talk we'll explore writing JavaScript that is performant, small and can leverage micro frameworks that are surprisingly feature rich.

Hacking Modern Multicopters

Building small multi-motor vehicles is cheaper and easier than it's ever been and with modern controllers and radios it's never been more hackable. In this 5 minute talk Chris will explain the basics and show you how to get off the ground.
